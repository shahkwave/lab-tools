How coat color genetics work in Labradors:
I built this tool to help streamline color prediction outcomes, but it's still in the testing phase. I haven't had the chance to fact-check every outcome yet, and AI can sometimes get things wrong. Please treat all results as suggestions rather than facts. Use at your own risk—and if you spot a bug, let me know!
B locus controls pigment type — B (dominant) = black pigment; b (recessive) = chocolate pigment. A dog needs two copies of b (bb) to be chocolate.
E locus controls whether that pigment is expressed. Two copies of e (ee) suppress color expression entirely, producing a yellow coat regardless of the B locus.
Note: Fox Red is simply a deep shade of yellow (ee genotype). Dilute colors — Silver, Charcoal, Champagne — are caused by the dilute gene at the D locus and are not a recognized color for the breed. Therefore, this tool is only for Labradors with official recognized colors.
